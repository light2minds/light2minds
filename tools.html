🌊 Sensory Tool Recommendations

These are general sensory tool categories recommended by occupational therapists. Always consult an OT for individualized recommendations for your child.

🦺

Weighted Vests & Blankets

Deep pressure tools that provide proprioceptive input to help calm and regulate children who seek heavy work or have trouble with sensory modulation.

Deep Pressure
🎧

Noise-Canceling Headphones

Helpful for children with auditory hypersensitivity — in classrooms, grocery stores, or any high-stimulation environment. Available in child sizes.

Auditory
🪑

Wobble Seats & Cushions

Provides vestibular and proprioceptive input for children who need movement to focus. Great for classroom or homework desk use.

Vestibular
🎾

Fidget Tools

Textured fidgets, stress balls, and hand spinners provide tactile input and help channel sensory-seeking behavior in a controlled way during learning activities.

Tactile
🌡️

Oral Sensory Tools

Chew necklaces, vibrating oral tools, and chewy tubes for children who are oral sensory seekers — a safer, socially appropriate alternative to chewing clothing or objects.

Oral
🫧

Sensory Bins & Play Materials

Sand, water, kinetic sand, slime, and rice bins provide controlled tactile and proprioceptive exploration — often used in sensory diets prescribed by OTs.

Tactile
💡

Visual Sensory Tools

Sunglasses for light sensitivity, visual timers (like Time Timer), calm-down glitter jars, and visual schedules to reduce visual overstimulation and support transitions.

Visual
🛏️

Calming Corner Essentials

A designated, low-stimulation area with soft lighting, a bean bag or crash pad, weighted lap pad, noise-canceling headphones, and a feelings chart for self-regulation.

Self-Regulation
Download: Sensory Profile Questionnaire — help identify your child's sensory profile to share with their occupational therapist.
